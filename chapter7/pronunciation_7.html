Pronunciation Tip

The Definite Article in Fluent Speech/Reading (Part 2)

Shortening of Long Vowel before الـ

Review the Pronunciation section of Chapters 5 and 6 in preparation for the following tips. This section concludes some of the basic rules you will need to pronounce the definite article in fluent speech and reading.  As your knowledge of the language expands you will learn how to vowel all the words that precede الـ and will be able to observe the linking rule throughout.

Long vowel shortening before الـ
Long vowels that occur before الـ become short.  As usual, with moon letters, the vowel is then followed by the لـ and with sun letters the الـ is elided altogether.

Example with a moon letter:
 في البيت

Example with a sun letter:
 إلى الشارع  

Notice how in the moon letter example, the long vowel ي   is shortened to a kasra while in the sun letter example, the alif maqSuura is shortened to a fatHa.

 

Practice
Listen carefully to the shortened long vowels preceding الـ.  Practice saying the full sentences aloud.

۱- نحن نسافر دائمًا إلى القاهرة. MeaningWe always travel to Cairo.
۲- عمّته تعمل في الجامعة. MeaningHis (paternal) aunt works at the university.
۳- أختي الصغيرة لا تحبّ الدراسة. MeaningMy youngest sister does not like studying.

Speak to Your Instructor
Practice listening and saying the following sentences in preparation for the chat session where you will be asked to read them aloud to your instructor.

۱- عمّ مها يسكن في الإمارات. MeaningMaha's (paternal) uncle lives in the United Arab Emirates.
۲- كتابي الجديد على الكرسيّ. MeaningMy new book is on the chair.
۳- هذا الطالب يحبّ الفيزياء. MeaningThis student likes physics.
٤- والدتي تدرّس التاريخ في النهار وتدرس الكمبيوتر في المساء. MeaningMy mother teaches history during the day and studies computing in the evening.
٥- أنا لا أحبّ الرطوبة العالية. MeaningI do not like high (levels of) humidity.
