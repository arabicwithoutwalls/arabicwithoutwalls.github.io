tasharrafna تشرّفنا literally means "We are honored."

ism اسم means "name." The -i ي- at the end of ismi اسمي is the possessive pronoun "my."

maa ما (as the vocabulary list shows) means "what" and ism اسم means "name." The -ak ك-َ at the end of ismak اسمَك is the possessive pronoun "your (male);" whereas the -ik ك-ِ at the end of ismik اسمِك is the possessive pronoun "your (female)."

kayf كيف means "how." naquul نقول is the we conjugation of the verb "to say."

maadha ماذا means what and yacani يعني is the "you (male)" conjugation of the verb "to mean." (You may have noticed that maadha ماذا varies from the word for "what" that is presented in the vocabulary list. This is because there are two words for the interrogative "what" in Arabic: maa ما and maadha ماذا. maa ما is used in questions without verbs, and maadha ماذا is used in questions that contain verbs.)
