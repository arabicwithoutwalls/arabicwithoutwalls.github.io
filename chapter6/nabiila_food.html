Video Activity

أعشق تلك الأكلات

In this exercise, you will meet نبيلة. She is from Alexandria, a port city in Egypt. Listen to نبيلة discuss some of her favorite foods. With that in mind, watch the video.

Vocabulary:
تلك : those, this (feminine singular)
ورق العنب : photo needed!!!
كوشري : photo needed!!!
فول : photo needed!!!
طعمية : photo needed!!!

 


She says أفضّل. This literally means, "I like" or "I prefer."

She uses this word twice. You may recognize the root from a similar word you know: مفضّل (favorite).

What word does نبيلة first use to talk about foods she likes? Listen for the verb she uses.

   Hint   Answer

If you have not yet clicked on the links in the vocabulary to see the different foods نبيلة mentions, you should do so now. Have you ever tried any of these foods? If so, how did you like them? If not, which items would you like to try?

Listen to the video again to answer the following questions:



فلافل

a. نبيلة mentions another word for طعمية. What is it?

   Answer

كوشري وفول وطعمية

b. نبيلة describes certain foods as شعبية. This word does not have a direct English translation. It generally means "popular," but with other connotations such as "national," "folksy," or "of the people." What three dishes does نبيلة describe using this word?

   Answer


Her favorite foods. At the end, she summarizes her feelings about all the foods she mentioned. The word أعشق captures well how she feels--she really loves these foods. The verb is عشق - يعشق "to love passionately."

From the context, you might be able to guess the meaning. What has she been talking about throughout the video?

c. What does نبيلة mean when she says أعشق تلك الأكلات?

   Hint   Answer

Now that you have learned how to talk about favorite foods, seek opportunities to practice speaking. With a speaking partner, talk about foods you like or dislike. Play a game where one partner describes a food (most likely by listing its ingredients or the type of meal in which it is eaten) and the other partner guesses what food the first partner is describing. In addition, try to sample some of the foods that نبيلة has mentioned. Perhaps there is a restaurant or grocery near you that offers Middle Eastern dishes. If you can find these foods, and focus on what they are called in Arabic as you eat them, you will be unlikely to forget the dishes or their names.
